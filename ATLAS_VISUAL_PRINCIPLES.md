# Atlas Visual Principles

**Version:** 1.0 — Ratified

A practical visual and functional brief — not philosophy, not implementation. Any designer or engineer should be able to read this in two minutes and know what every future decision must satisfy.

**1. The screen is a frame, not an application.**
It is the window through which Atlas becomes visible — not a control panel, not a piece of software to operate. Nothing shown exists merely for the screen. Everything must derive from real company state, real activity, or Atlas's genuine judgment — never invented, never staged, never present purely for visual effect.

**2. Atlas has no location. The heart does.**
Atlas is distributed across every department, agent, decision and memory the company holds. It has no single home. The heart is the one fixed point where that distributed intelligence becomes recognizable and addressable — a point of contact, not a container.

**3. The heart is for presence and conversation, never for reporting.**
No number. No score. No chart. No status line. Its only functions: to be visibly present, and to be the place the CEO will eventually speak to. The instant it displays a metric, it becomes a widget and stops being the heart.

**4. The company exists around the heart as one continuous space.**
No panel, section or region stands independently. Every element's position is defined relative to the heart — proximity and prominence signal how close something currently is to what matters, not which product or system it "belongs" to.

**5. Required domains, visible around Atlas:**
KPIs · Departments · Employees / agents · Roadmap · Company Health (with a graph) · Available AI tools · CEO Inbox · Companies · Bugs and blockers. Each is an expression of the same intelligence, placed by relevance — not filed into a fixed category grid.

**6. No vertical scrolling in the primary desktop experience.**
The full state of the company is visible in one view, at once, on a standard desktop viewport. If something doesn't fit, it is summarized to fit. Nothing important is ever pushed below the fold.

**7. Structure stays spatially recognizable.**
Structure determines where a domain belongs. The general position of each domain stays consistent day to day — a CEO finds "Inbox" or "Departments" from memory, without searching.

**8. Meaning determines emphasis, proximity, scale and weight — within that structure.**
Where a domain lives is fixed by structure (principle 7). How much space, prominence or weight it currently claims within that position is decided entirely by significance — never by a fixed template, never by whatever data happens to exist. If nothing meaningful changed, nothing should look different.

**9. No area may feel like an independent SaaS widget.**
Test: crop any region out of the screen. If it still means exactly the same thing on its own, it has become a card. If it loses meaning once separated from its relationship to the heart, it is correctly part of the space.

**10. Every visible change must correspond to something real.**
No motion, resizing, or brightening without a real, traceable cause in state, activity, or significance. No animation added for pacing, polish, or rhythm.

**11. The primary experience must answer three questions:**
How is our company doing? What should we build today? How does that contribute to the growth we are trying to achieve? Every visible element should trace back to one of these three. If it answers none of them, it does not belong in the primary view.

**12. The reaction should be disbelief that software can feel this coherent — earned, not staged.**
That reaction must come from truth: real data, real coherence, real judgment, visibly working together. Never from manufactured motion standing in for it.

---

**Never**

- Never turn the heart into a KPI widget.
- Never animate it merely to simulate life.
- Never build a grid of independent cards.
- Never force the CEO to scroll to find the company's primary state.
- Never let visual spectacle outrank meaning.
- Never show "live" unless the data is genuinely live.
